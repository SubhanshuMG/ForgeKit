# ForgeKit Trademark Policy

## Overview

"ForgeKit" and the ForgeKit logo are trademarks of the ForgeKit project maintainers.

The Apache License 2.0 under which ForgeKit source code is distributed **does not** include a license to use the ForgeKit trademark. This policy defines what is and is not permitted.

---

## What You CAN Do

You are free to:

- **Fork the code** and build your own tool based on ForgeKit, under the terms of Apache 2.0
- **Say "based on ForgeKit"** or "powered by ForgeKit" to accurately describe your project's origin
- **Write about ForgeKit** in articles, blog posts, talks, and documentation
- **Use the name in comparisons** (e.g. "similar to ForgeKit") that are accurate and not misleading
- **Contribute back** under your own name with proper attribution

---

## What You CANNOT Do

You may NOT:

- Name your fork or derived project "ForgeKit", "ForgeKit Pro", "ForgeKit Cloud", or any name that could be confused with the official project
- Use the ForgeKit logo or any variation of it without written permission
- Create a website, product, or service that implies official ForgeKit affiliation without permission
- Register domain names containing "ForgeKit" for a competing or related product
- Use "ForgeKit" in a company name, product name, or service name without written permission

---

## Forks and Derivatives

You are welcome to fork ForgeKit. If you do:

1. Rename your fork clearly so it is not confused with ForgeKit
2. Update the README to note that it is a fork and link back to the original
3. Keep the NOTICE file and Apache 2.0 copyright headers intact (required by Apache 2.0)
4. Do not represent your fork as the official ForgeKit project

---

## Requesting Permission

To request permission for uses not covered above, contact the maintainers via GitHub Discussions or open an issue tagged `trademark-request`.

We are generally friendly about legitimate uses, just ask first.

---

## Why This Policy Exists

Open source licenses like Apache 2.0 protect the code. Trademark protection protects the community: it ensures that when someone uses ForgeKit, they know they're getting the real thing, maintained to the project's standards of quality and security.

This policy is modeled after the trademark policies used by the Linux Foundation, Kubernetes, and Node.js projects.
